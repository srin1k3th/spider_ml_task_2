# Nutrition Guidelines for Health

## Core Principles

A healthy dietary pattern includes nutrient-dense foods and beverages across all food groups, in recommended amounts, and within calorie limits. The core elements of a healthy dietary pattern are:

- Vegetables of all types — dark green, red and orange, beans, peas, lentils, starchy, and other vegetables
- Fruits, especially whole fruit rather than juice
- Grains, at least half of which are whole grains
- Dairy, including fat-free or low-fat milk, yogurt, and cheese, or lactose-free versions and fortified soy beverages
- Protein foods including lean meats, poultry, eggs, seafood, beans, peas, lentils, nuts, seeds, and soy products
- Oils, including vegetable oils and oils in food such as seafood and nuts

## Key Dietary Recommendations

Limit foods and beverages higher in added sugars, saturated fat, and sodium, and limit alcoholic beverages:

- Added sugars: Less than 10% of calories per day from added sugars. For a 2,000 calorie diet, that is 200 calories or about 12 teaspoons of sugar.
- Saturated fat: Less than 10% of calories per day from saturated fat. Replace with unsaturated fats, particularly polyunsaturated fats.
- Sodium: Less than 2,300 mg per day for adults, and even less for children under 14. Most Americans consume about 3,400 mg per day, well above recommendations.
- Alcohol: Adults of legal drinking age can choose not to drink, or to drink in moderation — 2 drinks or less per day for men, 1 drink or less per day for women.

## Heart-Healthy Diet Patterns

### DASH Diet (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension)

The DASH eating plan is specifically designed to lower blood pressure:
- Rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and fat-free or low-fat dairy
- Includes fish, poultry, beans, nuts, and vegetable oils
- Limits foods high in saturated fat such as fatty meats, full-fat dairy, and tropical oils
- Limits sugar-sweetened beverages and sweets
- Sodium target: 1,500-2,300 mg per day

Clinical trials have shown DASH can lower systolic blood pressure by 8-14 mmHg.

### Mediterranean Diet

Characterized by high intake of olive oil, fruits, nuts, vegetables, and cereals, moderate intake of fish and poultry, and low intake of dairy products, red meat, processed meats, and sweets. Associated with reduced cardiovascular mortality and incidence of major cardiovascular events.

## Specific Nutrient Guidance

- Fiber: Adults should consume 25-30 grams per day. Most Americans consume only about 15 grams.
- Potassium: Adequate intake is 2,600 mg/day for women and 3,400 mg/day for men. Good sources include bananas, potatoes, spinach, and beans.
- Calcium: 1,000-1,200 mg/day for adults depending on age and sex.
- Vitamin D: 600-800 IU/day for adults. Many people may need supplementation, especially those with limited sun exposure.
- Iron: 8-18 mg/day depending on age and sex. Higher needs during pregnancy (27 mg/day).
- Omega-3 fatty acids: Eating 8 ounces of seafood per week (providing about 250 mg/day of EPA and DHA) is associated with reduced cardiac deaths among the general population.

## Weight Management Through Diet

Caloric balance is essential for maintaining a healthy weight. To lose weight, create a calorie deficit through a combination of reduced caloric intake and increased physical activity. A deficit of 500-750 calories per day typically results in about 1-1.5 pounds of weight loss per week. Extreme calorie restriction (below 1,200 calories for women, 1,500 for men) is not recommended without medical supervision.
